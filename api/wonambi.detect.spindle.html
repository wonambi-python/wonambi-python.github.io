Bases: object

Design spindle detection on a single channel.

Parameters:

  • method (str) ‚Äì one of the predefined methods

  • frequency (tuple of float) ‚Äì low and high frequency of spindle band

  • duration (tuple of float) ‚Äì min and max duration of spindles

  • merge (bool) ‚Äì if True, then after events are detected on every channel, events on different channels that are separated by less than min_interval will be merged into a single event, with ‚Äòchan‚Äô = the chan of the earlier-onset event.

toleranceÔÉÅ

during detection and prior to applying the duration criterion, candidate events separated by less than this time interval are merged. In this way, the detector becomes tolerant to short dips below the eligibility threshold (e.g. if the spindle power drops for a split second).

Type:

float

min_intervalÔÉÅ

after the duration criterion is applied, events separated by less than this interval are merged into a single event, with ‚Äòchan‚Äô = the chan of the earlier-onset event.

Type:

float

power_peaksÔÉÅ

for peak power statistics. ‚Äòpeak‚Äô or ‚Äòinterval‚Äô. If None, values will all be NaN

  • dat2 (ndarray(dtype='float')) ‚Äì second vector with data

Notes

double_butter implements an effective bandpass by applying a highpass, followed by a lowpass. This method reduces filter instability, due to underlying numerical instability arising from nyquist / freq at low freq.

Wavelets pass only absolute values already, it does not make sense to store the complex values.
